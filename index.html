Addressing Biases
in the Field of Animal Behaviour

Animal behaviour research is affected by researchers' assumptions and expectations,
impacting how the research is conducted, interpretation of behaviours,
research questions addressed, and taxonomic representation.
In addition, the STRANGE framework reveals biases in subject inclusion.

Animal behaviour, like other academic fields, is shaped by historical biases
influencing geographic coverage and whose research is recognised as influential.
Including diverse perspectives ensures new insights and robust science.

At the ASAB Winter Meeting 2024, we explore biases in animal behaviour research, and
discuss a comprehensive system change towards inclusivity and fairness. This involves
evaluating community needs and implementing explicit policies alongside implicit cultural changes.

We aim to publish a special issue with conference proceedings.
